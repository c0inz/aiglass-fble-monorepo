The operator approved a strategic meta-architecture for the project: a single umbrella domain (`sosatisfying.com`) with subpaths per niche (for example `sosatisfying.com/pellet-smokers/`, `sosatisfying.com/homelab-ups/`), with a hybrid migration plan to move any niche that clears roughly $20/month in affiliate revenue onto its own domain.

The operator approved the stack decision: Astro with MDX on Cloudflare Pages, rejecting Next.js. Rationale recorded: Astro ships zero JS by default, MDX is first-class, content collections support typed per-product frontmatter, and per-page islands allow selective interactivity (such as a sortable comparison table). All fifty sites are to live in one repository as fifty content collections under one deploy, one cache, and one analytics dashboard.

The operator approved a content production model that is human-led, AI-assisted, and human-edited. The human picks products and angle, AI drafts ~600-word pages, and the human edits for voice and adds personal anecdotes. Each page must contain at least one observation that could only come from real use of the product.

The operator approved an affiliate program mix using Amazon Associates as the default and adding direct affiliate programs where they pay better (the assistant cited 8–15% versus Amazon's roughly 3% as the comparison). The template definitions presented and not yet contradicted include:

- Four page types: homepage (decision page with hero, trust strip, top 3–5 picks, comparison table, mistakes, FAQ, final CTA), `/best/<thing>` SEO landing pages with embedded product cards, `/vs/<a>-vs-<b>` head-to-head comparisons with a winner verdict and decision matrix, and `/guides/<topic>` for mistakes, setup, and budget-vs-premium content with inline product cards.
- No category index, no tag pages, no archive, no `/blog/`.
